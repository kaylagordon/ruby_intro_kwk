Displaying Information in the Console

One of the most important things that our code will do is display information to our user. Maybe we want to show our user the current weather or what items our shop has for sale. In Ruby, we will display information to our users with the keywords puts and print. Let's look at some examples below:

Our Code:

puts 'Hello World!'

Our Code:

print 'Hello World!'

Output:

Hello World!

Output:

Hello World!

Key Point #1: In Ruby, the commands puts and print will both display information in the console.

The keywords puts and print seem really similar, right? Let's see how they differ:

Our Code:

puts 'Hello World!'
puts 'I hope you have a great day!'

Our Code:

print 'Hello World!'
print 'I hope you have a great day!'

Output:

Hello World!
I hope you have a great day!

Output:

Hello World!I hope you have a great day!

Key Point #2: The difference between puts and print is that when you use puts, Ruby adds a new line at the end of each statement. Ruby does not add a new line with the keyword print.
